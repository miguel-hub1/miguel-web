// Certifications

HTB Certified Penetration Testing Specialist (CPTS)

Hack The Box PASSED

The CPTS certification validates advanced penetration testing skills, covering the full spectrum of attack techniques from reconnaissance to post-exploitation. This hands-on certification requires demonstrating real-world hacking capabilities in a challenging practical exam environment.

📚 CPTS Exam Preparation Recommendations

The CPTS exam is no joke. It requires extensive preparation and hands-on practice. Here are my recommendations for anyone planning to take this certification:

🚀

Complete the HTB Academy Path

Finish the entire Penetration Tester job role path on HTB Academy. Don't skip any modules - each one covers essential techniques you'll need during the exam. Take detailed notes as you go.

🎯

Practice with Dante Pro Lab

Complete the Dante Pro Lab before attempting the exam. It simulates a real penetration testing engagement with multiple hosts and teaches you how to pivot through networks effectively.

💻

Complete the CPTS Machine List

Work through the official HTB machine list recommended for CPTS preparation. These machines are specifically selected to cover the exam's attack vectors and techniques.

📝

Practice Report Writing

The exam requires a professional penetration testing report. Practice writing detailed reports with proper formatting, executive summaries, technical findings, and remediation recommendations.

⏱

Time Management is Critical

The exam has a strict time limit. Practice working efficiently and know when to move on from a stuck point. Create a methodology checklist to ensure you don't miss anything.

📋

Maintain Organized Notes

Keep well-organized notes throughout your preparation. Use tools like Notion, Obsidian, or CherryTree. During the exam, document everything - you'll need it for the report.

⚠

Important: Don't underestimate the exam difficulty. Many people fail on their first attempt. Take your time with preparation, understand the concepts deeply rather than just following walkthroughs, and make sure you're comfortable with Active Directory attacks, web exploitation, and privilege escalation techniques.
